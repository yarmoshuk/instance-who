1 Introduction

Nutrition Survey

A system for reporting the results of nutrition surveys conducted in refugee settings

How does it work?

1.Collection

Data is collected on paper questionnaires and/or mobile phones using the UNHCR Standardised Expanded Nutrition Survey (SENS) guidelines.

2.Analysis

Data is analysed using

Epi-Info and ENA for SMART software.

3.Data Entry

Nutrition survey results are entered directly into Twine.

4.Review

Results are reviewed and approved at Regional and Headquarters level.

5.Action

Final indicators are visualized and shared with partners to inform public health action.

What is it?

The nutrition survey database is a system for reporting the results of nutrition surveys conducted in refugee settings.

What are the objectives?

The objectives of the Nutrition survey database are:
  • To maintain a record of all nutrition surveys conducted in refugee and displaced settings
  • To permit decision makers to easily access and analyse results and to monitor changes over time
  • To integrate the results of nutrition surveys with indicators from other sources (e.g.HIS),in order to give a more comprehensive understanding of the public health situation.

Is it appropriate for me?

The Nutrition survey database can be classified as follows:
Setting
 Appropriate for camp and urban settings
Operational Phase
 Appropriate for emergency and protracted settings
Income Setting
 Appropriate for low and middle/high income settings
Method
 Survey tool
Sector
 Collects public health,nutrition,food security and WASH data
Technology
 Entered directly into the web-browser
Unit of Measurement
 Measures indicators at the individual and household level

What does it collect?
  • The Nutrition survey database contains key indicators from Nutrition Surveys conducted in refugee settings from 1997 to present.
  • From 2012, the selection of indicators has been based on the new Standardised Expanded Nutrition Survey (SENS) guidelines for refugee populations.
In addition to a core Anthropometry and Health module,the SENS guidelines also include additional optional modules on:
  • Anaemia
  • Infant and Young Child Feeding (IYCF)
  • Food Security
  • WASH
  • Malaria.

Why is it important?
  • The prevalence of global and severe acute malnutrition in children aged 6-59 months are key indicators used to determine the severity of a humanitarian crisis.They are also core indicators used to monitor progress towards UNHCR's Global Strategic Priorities (GSPs).
  • Tracking of other indicators, such as prevalence of anaemia, also helps to strengthen the monitoring of public health and nutrition programmes.

Who should use it?
  • The results of Nutrition surveys should be uploaded into the Nutriiton Survey database by Survey coordinator.This will ensure that the results are entered as accurately as possible.
  • If this is not possible,then the UNHCR Nutrition focal point should take responsibility to entering the results.

How long will it take?
  • A nutrition survey takes on average 2 to 3 months to conduct (including planning, training, implementation, analysis and write up of results).

How often do I need to do it?
  • Nutrition surveys are normally conducted once a year. However, if indicated they can be conducted more frequently (e.g. if there is a major change in the nutritional situation or during a nutritional emergency).
  • A nutrition survey should ideally be conducted at the same time each year.
  • A nutrition survey should be conducted within two months of the start of a new operation or a major new refugee influx.

What are the limitations?
  • A Nutrition Survey is a large and often expensive exercise to conduct,and needs to be done with the supervision of experts in order to achieve high quality results. This means that it is not always feasible to conduct as often as is needed due to limited resources.

What other tools are relevant?
  • The results of the Nutrition survey should be routinely compared with HIS indicators.The triangulation of data in this way helps to validate indicators collected via different sources,and also helps to give a more comprehensive understanding of the public health situation in a given location.

Who will review the reports?
  • At country level,the Nutrition survey results will be reviewed and approved by the Nutrition survey team leader or the UNHCR nutrition survey coordinator.
  • Next,they will be reviewed at Regional and Headquarters levels.
  • The final nutrition survey results will be made publicly available after they have been approved at all other levels.

How is the quality of data assured?

The quality of data in the nutrition survey database is upheld in the following ways:
  • All nutrition surveys conducted in refugee settings should follow the SENS guidelines, which promote high quality data collection and analysis.
  • The data entry form where data is first entered in Twine has validation rules to only accept appropriate values.
  • The final nutrition survey results are reviewed at country,regional and headquarters level to ensure results are carefully examined and corrected where necessary.
