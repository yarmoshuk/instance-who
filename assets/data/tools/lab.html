1 Introduction

Laboratory Evaluation

A structured questionnaire to evaluate the quality of laboratory services in primary health care facilities in refugee settings

How does it work?

1.Collection

Data is collected on paper questionnaires and/or mobile tablets.

2.Data Entry

Data is entered into a mobile tablet.

3.Reporting

Data is submitted from the mobile tablet directly to Twine.

4.Analysis

Data is automatically analysed and converted into Laboratory Evaluation indicators.

5.Review

Results are reviewed and approved at Regional and Headquarters level.

6.Action

Final indicators are visualized and shared with partners to inform public health action.

What is it?

The laboratory evaluation is a structured questionnaire to evaluate the quality of laboratory services provided in primary health care facilities.

What are the objectives?

The objectives of the Laboratory evaluation are:
  • To identify gaps and develop targeted recommendations related to key domains of laboratory service management and service provision
  • To monitor changes over time
  • To mobilise additional training,resources or technical support
  • To document good practices.

Is it appropriate for me?

The Laboratory evaluation can be classified as follows:
Setting
 Appropriate for camp and urban settings
Operational Phase
 Appropriate for protracted settings
Income Setting

 Evaluation tool

Technology
 Entered into mobile tablets

 Measures indicators at the health facility level

What does it collect?

The Laboratory evaluation allocates scores to a laboratory in the following domains:
  • Workload
  • Utilities and Laboratory Structure
  • Biosafety,Hygiene and Security
  • Specimen Collection and Handling
  • Equipment and supplies
  • Reagents and supplies
  • Analysis and tests performed
  • Laboratory staff and working time
  • Reporting,analysis and communication
  • Quality assurance
  • Outbreak participation.

Why is it important?
  • The Laboratory evaluation is a core component ofUNHCR's Principles and Guidelines for Laboratory Services in UNHCR-Supported Primary Health Care Facilities.
  • This document can be downloaded fromhere.

Who should use it?
  • A Laboratory evaluation should be used by both UNHCR and health partners.The results should be shared and made available to laboratory staff to ensure they can learn and benefit from the recommendations.

How long will it take?
  • A Laboratory evaluation will take between 2 to 3 hours to complete.

How often do I need to do it?

After a baseline assessment has been conducted,follow-up assessments are recommended at the following intervals,depending on the results:
Score
Follow-up interval
<40% after 3 months
40–60% after 6 months
>60% after 1 year

What other tools are relevant?
  • The Laboratory evaluation complements the laboratory section found within the Balanced scorecard.
  • If the Balanced scorecard raises concerns about the quality of laboratory services,then the Laboratory evaluation offers a more in-depth tool to assess and understand where gaps may exist.

Who will review the reports?
  • At country level,the laboratory evaluations are reviewed and approved by the UNHCR health coordinator.
  • Next,they will be reviewed at Regional and Headquarters levels.
  • The final laboratory evaluations will be made publicly available after they have been approved at all other levels.

How is the quality of data assured?

The quality of data in each Laboratory evaluation is upheld in the following ways:
  • The questions for the mobile tablets have skip patterns to ensure that only relevant questions are asked.
  • The indicators are automatically calculated by a database to speed up analysis and reduce the possibility of human error.
  • Each Laboratory evaluation goes through a review process at country,regional and headquarters level to ensure results are carefully examined and corrected where necessary.

Where can I get help?
