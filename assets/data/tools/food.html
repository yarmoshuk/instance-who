1 Introduction

Food Aid Report

A tool to monitor the availability and composition of food aid rations, and the quality of food aid programmes, in refugee operations.

How does it work?

1.Collection

Food distributions are monitored each quarter at camp level.

2.Data Entry

Data is entered into an Excel sheet.

3.Reporting

Data is submitted from the Excel sheets directly to Twine.

4.Analysis

Excel data is automatically analysed and converted into indicators.

5.Review

Results are reviewed and approved at Regional and Headquarters level.

6.Action

Final indicators are visualized and shared with partners to inform action.

What is it?

The Food Aid report monitors the availability and composition of food aid rations, and the quality of food aid programmes, in refugee operations.

What are the objectives?

The objectives of the Food Aid report are:
  • To monitor the availability and composition of food aid rations, and the quality of food aid distributions.
  • To track past or expected breaks in the food distribution pipelines.
  • To review the frequency of food basket monitoring and post distribution monitoring.
  • To maintain an accurate calendar of Joint Assessment Missions (JAMs).

Is it appropriate for me?

The Food Aid report can be classified as follows:
Setting
 Appropriate for camp and urban settings
Operational Phase
 Appropriate for protracted and emergency settings
Income Setting
 Appropriate for low and middle/high income settings
Method
 Monitoring tool
Sector
 Collects food security data
Technology
 Entered into an Excel sheet
Unit of Measurement
 Measures indicators at the community level

What does it collect?

Data is collected on the following types of food distribution:
  • General dry food distribution
  • Complementary (non-fresh) food distribution
  • Complementary (fresh) food distribution
  • Blanket feeding
  • Supplementary feeding.

All sections are optional and can be configured according to the types of food distribution that are provided in each operation.

Why is it important?
  • The Food Aid report is a key tool for monitoring the availability and accessibility of food distributed in refugee settings.

Who should use it?
  • The Food Aid report should be completed primarily by Programme Officers,with inputs from other colleagues as needed (such as nutrition, food security or field staff).

How long will it take?
  • The Food Aid report should take approximately 1-2 hours to complete.

How often do I need to do it?
  • The Food Aid report should be completed every quarter.

What other tools are relevant?
  • The Food Aid report should be interpreted alongside other food security and nutrition indicators collected in the Health Information System (HIS) and the Nutrition Survey database.

Who will review the reports?
  • Food Aid reports will be reviewed and approved at country level by the UNHCR Programme Officer.
  • Next,they will be reviewed at Regional and Headquarters levels.
  • The final Food Aid reports will be made publicly available every quarter after they have been approved at all other levels.

How is the quality of data assured?

The quality of data in the Food Aid report is upheld in the following ways:
  • The Excel sheets where data is first entered have validation rules to only accept appropriate values.
  • Each Food Aid report is reviewed at country and regional level to ensure results are carefully examined and corrected where necessary.

Where can I get help?
  • For more help and support,please contact HQHIS@unhcr.org
