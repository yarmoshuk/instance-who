1 Introduction

Maternal Death Review

A system for reporting the findings and recommendations of reviews into maternal deaths in refugee settings

How does it work?

1.Collection

Every maternal death in a refugee setting is reviewed using standardized guidelines and reporting format.

2.Data Entry

The findings and conclusions of each maternal death review are entered directly into Twine.

3.Review

Results are reviewed and approved at Regional and Headquarters level.

4.Action

Final indicators are visualized and shared with partners to inform public health action.

What is it?

The maternal death review is a tool for reporting the findings and recommendations of investigations into maternal deaths in refugee settings.

What are the objectives?

The objectives of the maternal death review are:
  • To highlight all elements that contributed to the death in order to work on those elements and avoid similar deaths in the future
  • To understand the circumstances around the death
  • To precisely identify the cause of death
  • To improve the access and quality of safe motherhood programming (antenatal care,delivery,postnatal care,post abortion care),with particular focus on Emergency Obstetric Care to prevent future maternal and neonatal morbidity and mortality.

Is it appropriate for me?

The maternal death review can be classified as follows:
Setting
 Appropriate for camp and urban settings
Operational Phase
 Appropriate for emergency and protracted settings
Income Setting
 Appropriate for low and middle/high income settings
Method
 Tool for reporting immediate events
Sector
 Collects RH/HIV/AIDS data
Technology
 Entered directly into the web-browser
Unit of Measurement
 Measures indicators at the individual level

What is a maternal death?
  • A maternal death is the death of a woman while pregnant or within 42 days of the end of pregnancy,irrespective of the duration and the site of the pregnancy,from any cause related to or aggravated by the pregnancy or its management,but not from accidental or incidental causes.

What is maternal death review?
  • A Maternal death review is a systematic and structured process to understand the medical and social reasons that contributed to a maternal death.

What does it collect?

The maternal death review collects information on:
  • The pregnancy,including previous obsetric history
  • The death,including location,main symptoms and summarised history of events
  • An analysis of the key factors that contributed to the delays in seeking,reaching and/or receiving care
  • Conclusions and recommendations on how similar deaths could be prevented in future.

Why is it important?
  • A Maternal death review provides a rare opportunity for a group of health staff,family and community members to learn from a tragicâ€“and often preventable-event.

Who should use it?
  • A maternal death review should be carried out by a designated team of UNHCR,implementing partners and the Ministry of Health wherever possible.

How long will it take?
  • A comprehensive maternal death review will take 4-5 days to complete-including the planning,training,implementation and then write up and analysis of results.

How often do I need to do it?
  • Every maternal death (refugee or a national) that occurs at home or a health centre;on the road to nearest health facility/referral hospital,or at a referral health facility should be systematically reviewed

What are the limitations?

Maternal deaths are often under-reported in refugee settings.To ensure that there is good coverage of reporting,the following best practices are advised:
  • Have a SOP for reporting maternal deaths and initiation of the maternal death audit process.
  • Make health workers sign a code of conduct that they will report maternal deaths within 24 hours of occurrence/coming to their attention.
  • Make it necessary for maternal and child health(MCH)staff to have an updated database of all pregnant woman in each camp.This should include address information,and should be updated as the delivery occurs and as each woman safely reaches 42 days post-partum.
  • Community health workers should maintain a database of pregnant woman in their coverage area and inform health facility of any maternal deaths.
  • Entrust responsibility of reporting maternal deaths also to religious leaders and health sector committees.

What other tools are relevant?
  • The maternal death review reports should be used in conjunction with the HIS,to verfiy that each maternal death reported in HIS is also reviewed and followed up with a detailed maternal death report.

Who will review the reports?
  • Maternal death reports will be reviewed and approved at country level by the UNHCR health coordinator.
  • Next,they will be reviewed at Regional and Headquarters levels.
  • The final maternal death reports will remain internal and will not be made publicly available.

How is the quality of data assured?

The quality of data in each maternal death report is upheld in the following ways:
  • The data entry form where data is first entered in Twine has validation rules to only accept appropriate values.
  • The final maternal death report is reviewed at country,regional and headquarters level to ensure results are carefully examined and corrected where necessary.

Where can I get help?
  • For more help and support,please contact HQHIS@unhcr.org
