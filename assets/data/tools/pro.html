1 Introduction

Prospective Surveillance

A tool to monitor refugee access to health services in urban settings,by following a cohort of individual refugees

How does it work?

1.Collection

A cohort of individual refugees is followed each month to record experiences of accessing and utilizing health services in urban areas.

2.Data Entry

Data is entered into a mobile tablet.

3.Reporting

Data is submitted from the mobile tablet directly to Twine.

4.Analysis

Data is automatically analysed and converted into indicators.

5.Review

Results are reviewed and approved at Regional and Headquarters level.

6.Action

Final indicators are visualized and shared with partners to inform public health action.

What is it?

A system to monitor refugee access to health services in urban settings,by following a cohort of individual refugees.

What are the objectives?

The objectives of Prospective surveillance are:
  • To monitor trends in how urban refugees access and utilise health services over time.
  • To describe the challenges and obstacles faced by urban refugees when accessing health services.
  • To assess the effectiveness of health information,education and communication messaging in urban settings.

Is it appropriate for me?

Prospective surveillance can be classified as follows:
Setting
 Appropriate for urban settings
Operational Phase
 Appropriate for protracted settings
Income Setting
 Appropriate for low and middle/high income settings
Method
 Monitoring tool
Sector
 Collects Public Health,RH/HIV/AIDS and nutrition data
Technology
 Entered into a mobile tablet
Unit of Measurement
 Measures indicators at the individual level

What does it collect?

Prospective surveillance consists of two main components:
Baseline questionnaire
  • This is a questionnaire to collect information on household demographics,knowledge of health access rights,and key health conditions.
Medical event questionnaire
  • Each month every family is followed up to record information on any medical events that occurred since the last follow-up.This can be done either via face to face interviews or over the telephone.

Why is it important?
  • Prospective surveillance offers a unique refugee perspective on access to health care in urban areas.Wherever refugees choose to access services,this tool will permit UNHCR and partners to record information about that experience.

Who should use it?
  • Due to the intensive nature of the data collection,and need to conduct individual interviews with refugees,prospective surveillance will primarily be implemented and maintained by UNHCR.However,the results should also be shared with implementing partners working in urban health programmes,so they can benefit from and act upon the results.

How long will it take?
  • The interviews for propspective surveillance should take between 1-2 days to complete each month.The original baseline interviews may take a little longer.If the interviewing takes longer than this,then the sample size and/or number of interviewers that are available should be reviewed.

How often do I need to do it?
  • Prospective surveillance is designed to be conducted regularly every month,to obtain information on new health events that have occurred among the cohort that is under surveillance.

What are the limitations?
  • Prospective surveillance follows individual refugees over time using interviews.This is very time consuming and requires a lot of resources to conduct in an operation.This may not be a feasible data collection method for all urban operations.
  • It is important to conduct Prospective surveillance regularly at least once a month,as otherwise the time lag between the interviews can led to responder bias.
  • For practical purposes,only a limited number of refugees can be followed each month.It is recommended that a maximum of 80 households are selected.This low sample size means that the results need to be interpreted carefully and cannot necessarily be generalized to the entire refugee population.However,they can give important indications about the main challenges of refugee health access within a population.

What other tools are relevant?
  • Prospective surveillance is designed to be used alongside the Balanced Scorecard and the Urban HIS,to provide a comprehensive understanding of health service access,utilization and quality in urban settings.

Who will review the reports?
  • At country level,the prospective surveillance questionnaires will be reviewed and approved by the UNHCR health coordinator.
  • Next,they will be reviewed at Regional and Headquarters levels.
  • The final prospective surveillance factsheets will be made publicly available after they have been approved at all other levels.

How is the quality of data assured?

The quality of data in each Prospective surveillance report is upheld in the following ways:
  • The questions for the mobile tablets have skip patterns to ensure that only relevant questions are asked.
  • The indicators are automatically calculated by a database to speed up analysis and reduce the possibility of human error.
  • Each prospective surveillance report goes through a review process at country,regional and headquarters level to ensure results are carefully examined and corrected where necessary.
