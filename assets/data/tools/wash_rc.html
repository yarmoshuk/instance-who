1 Introduction

WASH Report Card

A tool for monitoring key water,sanitation and hygiene(WASH)indicators at household and community level in refugee camps

How does it work?

1.Collection

Data is collected at community and household level in refugee camps.

2.Data Entry

Data is entered into an Excel sheet.

3.Reporting

Data is submitted from the Excel sheets directly to Twine.

4.Analysis

Excel data is automatically analysed and converted into WASH indicators.

5.Review

Results are reviewed and approved at Regional and Headquarters level.

6.Action

Final indicators are visualized and shared with partners to inform public health action.

What is it?

The WASH report card monitors key water,sanitation and hygiene indicators at household and community level in refugee camps.

What are the objectives?

The objectives of the WASH report card are:
  • To monitor trends in access and utilisation of water over time at household level.
  • To monitor trends in availability and quality of water over time at community level.

Is it appropriate for me?

The WASH report card can be classified as follows:
Setting
 Appropriate for camp settings
Operational Phase
 Appropriate for protracted settings
Income Setting
 Appropriate for low and middle/high income settings
Method
 Monitoring tool
Sector
 Collects WASH data
Technology
 Entered into an Excel sheet
Unit of Measurement
 Measures indicators at the household and community level

What does it collect?

The WASH report card collects information on two groups of indicators:
Access indicators
  • Estimating litres per person per day
  • Testing chlorinated water
  • Testing unchlorinated water
  • Measuring toilet coverage.
Household indicators
  • Water collection
  • Water storage
  • Hygiene practices.

Why is it important?
  • WASH is a strategic priority for UNHCR and the WASH report card is a key tool to inform evidence-based decision making in this sector.

Who should use it?
  • The UNHCR WASH Officer and/or the WASH implementing partner are responsible for submitting the WASH report card each month.Results should also be shared with colleagues from other related sectors in Health and Nutrition.

How long will it take?
  • Access indicators in the WASH report card should take around 1-2 hours to compile each month.
  • Household level indicators require a separate methodology which should take 1-2 days to complete each month.

How often do I need to do it?
  • The indicators in the WASH report card should all be reported on a monthly basis.

What are the limitations?
  • The Household indicators within the WASH Report card rely on a sampling methodology that is still under review.It is being field tested to assess its ability to collect representative data and its appropriateness as a regular data collection tool in all field operations

What other tools are relevant?
  • The WASH report card is designed to be interpreted alongside other key data from the Health Information System (HIS).
  • Trends of diseases related to hygiene and sanitation conditions in camps (for example the incidence of watery diarrhoea,bloody diarrhoea,skin disease,eye disease and acute malnutrition) are shown in the WASH report card to support the interpretation of WASH indicators.

Who will review the reports?
  • WASH report cards are reviewed and approved at country level by the UNHCR or implementing partner WASH focal point.
  • Next,they will be reviewed at Regional level.
  • No Headquarters approval is needed for routine WASH reports.
  • The final WASH report cards will be made publicly available after they have been approved at all other levels.

How is the quality of data assured?

The quality of data in the WASH report card is upheld in the following ways:
  • The Excel sheets where data is first entered have validation rules to only accept appropriate values.
  • The indicators are automatically aggregated in a database,to reduce the possibility of human error.
  • Each WASH report card is reviewed at country and regional level to ensure results are carefully examined and corrected where necessary.
  • A data quality algorithm is automatically run on each report,and feedback provided to countries on the timelines,completeness and quality of data in their WASH reporting system.

Where can I get help?
  • For more help and support,please contact HQHIS@unhcr.org
