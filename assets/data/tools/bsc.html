1 Introduction

Balanced Scorecard

A set of 5 instruments designed to assess the quality of care provided in primary health care facilities

How does it work?

1.Collection

Data is collected on paper questionnaires and/or mobile tablets.

2.Data Entry

Data is entered into a mobile tablet.

3.Reporting

Data is submitted from the mobile tablet directly to Twine.

4.Analysis

Data is automatically analysed and converted into Balanced Scorecard indicators.

5.Review

Results are reviewed and approved at Regional and Headquarters level.

6.Action

Final indicators are visualized and shared with partners to inform public health action.

What is it?

The Balanced Scorecard is a set of 5 instruments designed to assess the quality of care provided in primary health care facilities.

What are the objectives?

The objectives of the Balanced Scorecard are:
  • To identify gaps and develop targeted recommendations related to key domains of health service management and service provision
  • To monitor changes over time
  • To mobilise additional training,resources or technical support
  • To document good practices.

What does it collect?

The Balanced Scorecard allocates scores to a health facility in the following domains:
  • Services provided
  • Staffing and coverage
  • Equipment and supplies
  • Quality of care
  • Health worker and patient satisfaction.

Why is it important?
  • The Balanced Scorecard is the core tool available to monitor quality of health care in UNHCR health programmes.

Who should use it?
  • The Balanced Scorecard should be used by both UNHCR and health partners.The results should be shared and made available to all staff to ensure they can learn and benefit from the recommendations.

How long will it take?
  • A comprehensive Balanced Scorecard assessment using all five instruments will take approximately 2 days to complete.

What are the limitations?
  • Some Balanced Scorecard indicators are weighted differently and can be harder to meet than others.Therefore,the results of any assessment should be interpreted carefully to understand the reasons why certain indicators failed.
  • The Balanced Scorecard is a facilty based tool and does not capture any data related to community-health activities.This needs to be assessed separately.

What other tools are relevant?
  • The Balanced Scorecard is designed to be interpreted alongside the HIS to give a comprehensive understanding of the quality of care provided in each camp.
  • In urban settings, the Balanced Scorecard is designed to be interpreted alongside the urban HIS and the Prospective Surveillance tools.

Who will review the reports?
  • At country level, the Balanced Scorecard will be reviewed and approved by the UNHCR health coordinator.
  • Next, they will be reviewed at Regional and Headquarters levels.
  • The final Balanced Scorecard will be made publicly available after they have been approved at all other levels.

How is the quality of data assured?

The quality of data in each Balanced Scorecard is upheld in the following ways:
  • The questions for the mobile tablets have skip patterns to ensure that only relevant questions are asked.
  • The indicators are automatically calculated by a database to speed up analysis and reduce the possibility of human error.
  • Each Balanced Scorecard goes through a review process at country,regional and headquarters level to ensure results are carefully examined and corrected where necessary.
