1 Introduction

Disease Outbreak Report

A system for reporting active disease outbreaks in refugee and displaced settings

How does it work?

1.Collection

Active disease outbreaks in refugee settings are monitored on a daily or weekly basis at field level.

to enable the outbreak to be monitored in a timely manner.

reporting format.

2.Data Entry

During an outbreak,the updated number of cases and deaths are entered into Twine each day or each week.

At the end of an outbreak,the key actions,lessons learned and conclusions are recorded.

3.Review

The final end of outbreak reports are reviewed and approved at Regional and Headquarters level.

4.Action

Final reports are shared and made available to partners to help inform the public health response to future outbreaks.

What is it?

Disease outbreak reports are designed to monitor and evaluate the public health response to disease outbreaks in refugee settings.

What are the objectives?

The objectives of the outbreak reporting system are:
  • To monitor the evolution of the outbreak over time and assess whether it is being brought under control.
  • To calculate key indicators such as case fatality ratio (CFR) and attack rate (AR).
  • To mobilise additional technical support to support the response if required.
  • To evaluate the effectiveness of the response and document lessons learned once the outbreak has ended.

Is it appropriate for me?

The outbreak reporting system can be classified as follows:
Setting
 Appropriate for camp settings
Operational Phase
 Appropriate for emergency and protracted settings
Income Setting
 Appropriate for low and middle/high income settings
Method
 Tool for reporting immediate events
Sector
 Collects public health data
Technology
 Entered directly into the web-browser
Unit of Measurement
 Measures indicators at the community level

What does it collect?

The outbreak reporting system collects information on:
During the outbreak
  • The diagnosis and laboratory status of the outbreak.
  • The number of cases and deaths reported each day or week,presented as an updated epidemiological curve.
At the end of the outbreak
  • A description of the outbreak by person,place and time.
  • An evaluation of the response;
  • Conclusions and recommendations on how the response to similar outbreaks could be improved in future.

Why is it important?
  • The over-riding goal during a disease outbreak is to protect the community and to reduce individual suffering.UNHCR aims to bring any outbreak under control as quickly as possible,limiting social disruption and associated death and illness.Effective communication using the disease outbreak reporting system is a key tool to achieve this goal.

Who should use it?
  • The UNHCR Health Coordinator is responsible for updating information on each outbreak into Twine.This is based on data received by partners,usually from disease line lists.

How long will it take?
  • During an outbreak,the updating of cases and deaths should not take more than 10 minutes to complete each day or week.
  • At the end of an outbreak,the final evaluation and submission of the end of outbreak report will take approximately 1 hour to complete.

How often do I need to do it?
  • Outbreaks can be monitored in Twine on either a daily or weekly basis.
  • It is recommended to monitor daily trends from the line lists,as this will provide more detailed epidemiological information to monitor the progression of the outbreak.
  • However,if this is not possible the weekly number of cases and deaths can be monitored instead.

What other tools are relevant?
  • The outbreak reporting system complements the HIS,as the HIS should help to detect the presence of a suspected outbreak from passive surveillance.Once confirmed,the active outbreak can then be monitored via the Disease Outbreak Reporting System.

Who will review the reports?
  • At country level,the UNHCR health coordinator is responsible for updating the outbreak each day or week.
  • When the outbreak is declared over,the UNHCR health coordinator is also responsible to completing the end of outbreak report.
  • After an outbreak is closed,the final report will also be reviewed by Regional and Headquarters levels.
  • The final outbreak reports will be made publicly available after they have been approved at all other levels.

How is the quality of data assured?

The quality of data in the outbreak report is upheld in the following ways:
  • The data entry form where data is first entered in Twine has validation rules to only accept appropriate values.
  • At the end of each outbreak,the final outbreak report is reviewed at country,regional and headquarters level to ensure results are carefully examined and corrected where necessary.

Where can I get help?
  • For more help and support,please contact HQHIS@unhcr.org
