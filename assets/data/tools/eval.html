1 Introduction

HIS Evaluation

A structured questionnaire to evaluate the quality of data collected in the Health Information System (HIS)

How does it work?

1.Collection

Data is collected on paper questionnaires and/or mobile tablets.

2.Data Entry

Data is entered into a mobile tablet.

3.Reporting

Data is submitted from the mobile tablet directly to Twine.

4.Analysis

Data is automatically analysed and converted into HIS Evaluation indicators.

5.Review

Results are reviewed and approved at Regional and Headquarters level.

6.Action

Final indicators are visualized and shared with partners to inform public health action.

What is it?

The HIS evaluation is a structured questionnaire to evaluate the quality of data collected in the Health Information System (HIS).

What are the objectives?

The objectives of the HIS evaluation are:
  • To identify gaps and develop targeted recommendations related to key area of HIS data collection and reporting
  • To monitor changes sover time
  • To mobilise additional training,resources or technical support
  • To document good practices.

Is it appropriate for me?

The HIS evaluation can be classified as follows:
Setting
 Appropriate for camp and urban settings
Operational Phase
 Appropriate for protracted settings
Income Setting
 Appropriate for low and middle/high income settings
Method
 Evaluation tool
Sector
 Collects Public Health,RH/HIV/AIDS and nutrition data
Technology
 Entered into mobile tablets
Unit of Measurement
 Measures indicators at the health facility level

What does it collect?

The evaluation allocates scores to an HIS in the following domains:
General sections
  • File management
  • Database management.
Technical sections

The technical sections follow the ten key modules of the HIS,and can be used selectively depending on the purpose and scope of the evaluation:
  1. Population
  2. Mortality
  3. Morbidity
  4. Inpatient department and Referral
  5. Laboratory
  1. Disease control
  2. EPI and Vitamin A
  3. Nutrition
  4. Reproductive Health
  5. HIV/AIDS.

Why is it important?
  • The HIS evaluation is the core tool available to monitor the quality of HIS data in UNHCR health programmes.

Who should use it?
  • An HIS evaluation should be conducted by both UNHCR and health partners.The results should be shared and made available to all staff involved in data collection and reporting,to ensure they can learn and benefit from the recommendations.

How long will it take?

The HIS evaluation tool can be customized to evaluate as many HIS modules as are required:
  • A fully comprehensive HIS evaluation–including all modules–will take approximately one day to complete.Shorter evaluations are possible if fewer modules are selected.

How often do I need to do it?

After a baseline assessment has been conducted,follow-up assessments are recommended at the following intervals,depending on the results:
Score
Follow-up interval
<40% after 3 months
40–60% after 6 months
>60% after 1 year

What other tools are relevant?
  • The HIS evaluation complements the HIS section found within the Balanced scorecard.If the Balanced scorecard raises concerns about the quality of HIS data,then the HIS evaluation offers a more in-depth tool to assess and understand where gaps may exist.

Who will review the reports?
  • At country level,the HIS evaluations will be reviewed and approved by the UNHCR health coordinator.
  • Next,they will be reviewed at Regional and Headquarters levels.
  • The final HIS evaluations will be made publicly available after they have been approved at all other levels.

How is the quality of data assured?

The quality of data in each HIS evaluation is upheld in the following ways:
  • The questions for the mobile tablets have skip patterns to ensure that only relevant questions are asked.
  • The indicators are automatically calculated by a database to speed up analysis and reduce the possibility of human error.
  • Each HIS evaluation goes through a review process at country,regional and headquarters level to ensure results are carefully examined and corrected where necessary.

Where can I get help?
  • For more help and support,please contact HQHIS@unhcr.org
